Privacy and Social Media at the Sir Bani Yas Forum

The Sir Bani Yas Forum is convened under the ‘Chatham House Rule’ of non-attribution and held in a strictly confidential setting. Therefore, participants are free to use any information received throughout the Forum, but neither the identity or affiliation of the speaker(s), that of any other participant, may be revealed.

The Forum believes it is essential that all participants and speakers are able to speak frankly and openly on the issues currently facing the Middle East in a strictly confidential environment. Through the implementation of the Chatham House Rule, the Forum seeks to encourage participants to engage in an environment of openness and trust.

Twitter and other social media platforms can be used by participants during and after the Sir Bani Yas Forum, but only in accordance with the Chatham House rules guiding the event.

Photos taken at the event should not include speakers or participants without their explicit permission and knowledge.
