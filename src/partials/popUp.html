In the Balance: Strategic Shifts in the Global Order

The world is moving toward multipolarity, characterized by competing centers of power with varying influence and size. At the same time, multilateral organizations are reasserting themselves in the Middle East, and new minilateral groupings are emerging as middle powers continue to proliferate. What does a multipolar world—versus the bipolar and unipolar international system that has been dominant for the last almost 80 years—mean for the Middle East? Does a multipolar world presage more conflict, more cooperation, or possibly both? Could the shifting global order lead to a new era of transactionalism instead of strategic engagement between states, and what would the long-term impact of this be? How might nonaligned states navigate this more complex world and growing great power competition? What is the role of Middle East states in this emerging global order?
